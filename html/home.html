Welcome to the Crop Rotation Project!

This website explores the importance of crop rotation in sustainable agriculture. Crop rotation involves alternating the types of crops grown on a specific piece of land over seasons. This practice helps improve soil health, reduce pests, and enhance crop yields.

Why Crop Rotation?

  • Improves Soil Health: Different crops contribute varying nutrients to the soil, preventing depletion.
  • Reduces Pest and Disease Build-up: Alternating crops disrupts the life cycles of pests and diseases.
  • Increases Biodiversity: Growing a variety of crops supports a diverse ecosystem.
  • Enhances Crop Yields: Healthy soil and reduced pest pressures lead to better yields.

How It Works?

Farmers typically rotate between legumes, grains, and other crops. For example, planting beans after corn can improve soil nitrogen levels, benefitting the subsequent crop.
